The Matcha Tokyo Omotesandō

Iced Matcha Latte

  • Location: 5 Chome-11-13 Jingumae, Shibuya City, Tokyo 150-0001, Japan
    1. Price: ¥680 YEN / $4.54 USD
    2. Rating: 5/5

I could write a book about this. THE MATCHA TOKYO sets a high bar for what Matcha latte is supposed to taste like. These are also one of the few stores that traditionally whisk the matcha in front of the customers to see, allowing full transparency into the creation of the drink. What makes this drink stand out? It is the rich umami flavor without any trace of bitterness or astringency. You can tell that the sourcing of these tea leaves goes through a meticulous process, also just by how the Matcha froths seamlessly, leaving a delicate foam layer on top. This quality is uncompromised even after the milk is poured, all because of the fine Matcha powder that it guarantees the smoothest sip. This drink is for matcha lovers to beginners alike. It is a luxurious, delicious experience for everyone. I don’t remember how many times I walked out of the hotel just to buy this drink. It is the BEST THING EVER.

Starbucks Reserve® Store - Ginza Marronnier-dori Street

Iced Matcha Latte

  • Location: Japan, 〒104-0061 Tokyo, Chuo City, Ginza, 3 Chome−7−3, Ginza Omi Bldg., 1F
    1. Price: ¥350 YEN / $2.30 USD
    2. Size: Short
    3. Rating: 2.5/5

You might be asking why one would order at Starbucks in Japan. In all honesty, the starbucks was decorated very nicely which was very attractive to me as a tourist coming in the spring (everything was pink!). As a result, I stepped in to order the matcha latte. To my surprise, there is a size smaller than the “tall” size that I was accustomed to– “short.” While this was a small drink, I could immediately taste the strong matcha flavor. However, it was slightly overpowered by some sweetness. I wanted to believe that the Starbucks in Japan would be different than the one I usually got at the U.S, but in all honesty, it was just a much more elevated version of the U.S matcha, as in it tastes more “authentic” in a way where there is more bitterness, but not enough for it to balance with the sweetness. For the price, I would say that this is pretty reasonable, especially for a size this small.

Yama

Hot Matcha Tea

  • Location: ZEX SHIROKANE 1F, 6-16-41 Shirokane, Minato-ku, Tokyo
    1. Price: ¥14,800 YEN / $97.73 USD
    2. Note: This price is part of a complete Omakase dinner.
    3. Rating: 5/5

This matcha was the definition of “authentic.” When the drink arrived, I immediately noticed how it was served straight off the “Chawan” it was whisked from, indicating the precise effort it took to create the drink by hand. I took a sip and felt the umami taste and the perfect blend of slight bitterness and savory tones. The creamy texture from the foam, whisked by the bamboo stick, proves the quality of the matcha and its freshness. Because it offers a ore vibrant green color, it introduces the delicate flavor profile that is unique and rare to be found anywhere else. Not only was the omakase excellent, topping it off with sweets (the sorbet shown on the top left of the screen) perfectly complemented the bitterness of the matcha.
