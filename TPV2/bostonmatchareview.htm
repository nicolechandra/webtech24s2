Blue Bottle Coffee

Iced Craft Matcha Latte

  • Location: Prudential Center, 800 Boylston St #25, Boston, MA 02199
    1. Price: $7.50
    2. Rating: 4/5

Once in a while, I think about this drink. I don’t know if it’s the branding, the presentation, or the ambiance and the entire experience of having this matcha, but it feels like a sunny, warm day. I would not say that the matcha itself is life changing or anything, but everything else surrounding it just seems to support it. The first sip feels very light, yet the subtle matcha flavor hits in the aftertaste. Because it is so light, it is almost watery, and depending on how I feel that day, I can either love it or hate it. The price, due to the company’s superb branding I must say, is quite expensive, but you gotta live a little sometimes. It is the perfect drink to regain focus and zen, pull up your laptop, and have a super productive day.

Matcha Maiko

Cheese Foam Matcha Latte

  • Location: 115 Jersey St, Boston, MA 02215
    1. Price: $8.30
    2. Rating: 3.5/5

I ordered the cheese latte thinking that it will be a good balance of bitter and sweet. I loved the creamy blend of the latte and the matcha. However, it was a little too sweet for my liking. Matcha has a naturally bitter taste so if you top it with milk AND cheese, it takes a heavier twist to the mouth, leaving a unique aftertaste of bitter and sweet. They definitely added more flavor drops or sweeteners, but it didn't overpower the bitter taste of matcha. In terms of pricing, this was definitely more to the pricier end, especially for a medium cup. I think this would be perfect for matcha beginners or those who don’t really enjoy the raw bitterness of matcha! Overall, it was very creamy yet refreshing.

Tatte

Iced Matcha Latte

  • Location: 1352 Boylston St, Boston, MA 02215
    1. Price: $4.50
    2. Rating: 3/5

My first impression of the matcha was, “Oh, that is a very light… green.” I’ve always thought that a generous amount of matcha would reach a point where the drink looks dark enough, but not too dark; dark, but vibrant green. More so than the color, it often speaks to the quality of the drink which I explain in “Why Ceremonial Grade Matcha?” Although the drink was bitter, the taste of the milk overpowered everything else. However, I love that they don’t add any extra sugars, so people can adjust the sweetness to their own liking. As for me, I really enjoyed how light and refreshing this drink is and despite them being heavier on the milk ratio, it still felt energizing and delicious!

Ogawa Coffee

Hot Matcha Latte

  • Location: 10 Milk St, Boston, MA 02108
    1. Price: $8.00
    2. Rating: 4/5

I really enjoyed this matcha. This latte has a very grassy note, followed with a pleasant bitterness and a lingering sweetness at the end. While they offer both sweetened and unsweetened versions, I ordered the unsweetened. You can tell a good matcha just by ordering the original flavor, and this was definitely it. Also, bonus points for the cool latte art! I came here during the weekend, so they were really busy and couldn’t make animal art. So, come during the weekdays! They will be able to make cute little rabbits, hearts, and animal shaped foam latte art that you can post on your Instagram stories.

Machi Machi

Panna Cotta Iced Matcha Latte

  • Location: 176 Harvard Ave, Allston, MA 02134
    1. Price: $10.65
    2. Rating: 3/5

For a price like this, one would expect for the taste of matcha to be more prominent. However, this was very heavy on the sweetness that it overwhelms the taste of matcha. However, the matcha had grassy undertones after the sip, but the panna cotta adds a unique addition to the flavor and texture of the drink. Because you have to chew a little bit of the panna cotta, it leaves a “heavier” finish compared to other lattes, but I would recommend this to those who are just starting out to like matcha, or could enjoy matcha with boba and other topping options.
