Gut Health

Catechins, a class of antioxidant that helps shield the body—including the gut—from oxidative stress and inflammation, are abundant in matcha. Epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), a particularly strong catechin found in matcha, has been investigated for its ability to promote digestive health and shield the lining of the gastrointestinal tract (National Library of Medicine).

How Matcha Helped My Digestion

As I mentioned in the About Me page, I grew up with an unhealthy obsession with coffee. It was until I found matcha that I realized how it has contributed to my overall health and appearance. From skin texture to bowel movements, I felt physically more energetic because I started cutting coffee and replacing it with my new favorite drink instead. As a result, I returned to the doctor and he told me how much better my sugar level regulations are. As I grew into womanhood, matcha also helped regulate my hormones and manage abdominal cramps, bloating, and even mood swings. Perhaps it is the “peaceful” aspect that comes along with its rich history, but its health factor also plays a huge role in contributing to one’s health– both physically and mentally.
