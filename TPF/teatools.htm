Tea Tools

These are the tea tools I use when making matcha. I just broke my chawan recently so it's not in the picture, but it should look something like this:

I would grab a scoop with the bamboo stick and put it inside the chawan. Sometimes I like to sift it to make the particles finer, but most of the time I skip this step. Before this step, I like to soften the bristles of the bamboo whisk by soaking it in hot water. But in reality, sometimes I just skip this step and pour a little bit of hot water over the matcha and whisk it. Once it is whisked with bubbles forming as a smooth foamy layer on top, you have the matcha base! You can add hot milk for a latte or add some ice, or just add more water for a more traditional taste. My all-time favorite is adding a little bit of grade A maple syrup for a little bit of sweetness and oat milk to finish my drink!

I would say these are the essentials. The green stand is to keep the bamboo whisk in shape after whisking and washing, so the next time you use it the bristles won't be ugly.
