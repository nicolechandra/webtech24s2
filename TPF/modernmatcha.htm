Modernized Matcha

Going back to my story on how I discovered matcha, it is so interesting to see how other people have grown and discovered matcha on their own. Influencers are beginning to promote its health benefits, even creating their own brands and claiming it to be “ceremonial grade” and “organic.” I have my own opinions on this: these terms don’t mean anything as far as quality goes. Just because you label these as “organic” or “ceremonial grade,” matcha in itself already has health benefits that act as the default, so claims such as these make me wonder: what other things may these influencers be emphasizing that is not already in matcha? Food for thought. For example, I recently discovered Emma Chamberlain’s company, Chamberlain Coffee.

Although Emma has reinvented how people can make matcha at the comfort of their own home, the ever-evolving ritual of matcha is constantly changing. Now, Matcha has become a “hollywood” trend that is inspired by hashtags such as the #itgirlaesthetic or the #cleangirl lifestyle. Although often associated with health and “fit” lifestyles, some influencers use this opportunity to capitalize on their brand image. At the same time, I am glad that matcha is getting the recognition it deserves. However, I hope people are more conscious in their choice of purchase, ensuring that these are ethically sourced with respect to its roots and tradition.

What Changed?

In its production techniques, traditional methods of shading, picking, and grinding matcha have seen technological advancements. In the past, stone mills were used to grind the tea leaves slowly to avoid heating and preserve flavor, some modern producers use advanced machinery that can maintain low temperatures to achieve the same effect without the labor-intensive process.

Although it was originally exclusive to Japan, matcha is now also cultivated in other countries like China and the U.S., adapting the traditional Japanese methods to different climates and soil conditions. As a result, its growing popularity worldwide only attracts more people to try this amazing antioxidant.

Beyond the traditional tea ceremony, matcha is now used in a variety of food and beverages. It's a popular ingredient in lattes, smoothies, desserts, and even savory dishes, which has been facilitated by its global popularity and the influence of health and wellness trends.
