Welcome to Nicole's Matcha Blog

Welcome to my blog! My name is Nicole Chandrawidjaja. This page is mostly dedicated to matcha, where I will take pictures and try matcha variations in different locations while reviewing everything from drinks to desserts! Whether you’re a matcha lover or if you’re just beginning to try it out, this blog will be covering its rich history while also documenting new matcha spots, how to make matcha, and is dedicated to those who want to nerd-out about the history and current trends of matcha! I will be grading it out of five. One is least recommended and five is highly recommended. Although we all have different preferences of matcha, I prefer my matcha to taste slightly earthy– bitter at first taste, sweet when you swallow. Thus, these are all ratings based on my own preference, but I will do my best to review each delicacy to the best of my ability.

How I Discovered Matcha

When I was eight, I developed a really strong, unhealthy addiction to coffee. This was when the Starbucks secret recipes were trendy and I wanted to try all the new frappuccinos, such as the “Butterbeer Harry Potter” drink or the “Pink Cotton Candy” drink. Because of this, I noticed that I would spend so much money and drink unnecessary amounts of sugar. My energy levels would crash and whenever exams started to hit, I drank coffee like I was working a 9 to 5, when I really only dealt with the multiplication table. While I was taking the exam, I remember trying a new espresso drink and felt my heart beating uncomfortably. I felt anxious. Eventually, this led to physical drawbacks: I was dehydrated and was diagnosed with low blood pressure at the age of eight. I wanted to slowly cut coffee and regain my energy without having to depend on a drink.
Summer came and my family and I flew to Japan for vacation. While I explored the Fushimi Inari Shrine in Kyoto, I found little market stalls that sold traditional household items. I stepped inside the store and saw a group of people sitting down to watch a woman use a bamboo whisk and meticulously pour hot water. I became curious and stepped inside, watching as she carefully scooped more green powder, added hot water, whisked it, and served it to the people around her. She gave me a warm cup of this green liquid, and I took a sip. I immediately felt peace. I enjoyed the smell of the drink and loved how it made me feel grounded, yet energized. I immediately asked my mom to purchase this new tea I learned known as “Matcha.”
